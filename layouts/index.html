Personal Injury Lawsuits

Personal injury lawsuits are against whichever entity, either a person or company, is at fault for your injury. Personal injury lawsuits require proof of negligence, meaning the person or company failed to take proper care in protecting you from harm.

Personal injury lawsuits end in either a settlement or verdict. Most cases settle outside of the courtroom. In some instances, they’re decided by a judge’s or jury’s decision.

Wrongful Death Claims

Wrongful death claims are personal injury cases where the victim has passed away due to the injuries or medical condition related to the case. A few facts about wrongful death claims:

  • Surviving loved ones (usually spouse or children) file on behalf of their deceased family member
  • Since the injury or medical condition resulted in death, the courts are favorable to the family and take into account their pain and suffering

Settlements

Settlements for personal injury cases are agreements between the victim and the negligent party to end the case. They usually involve a monetary payment from the negligent entity to the victim.

Settlements result in a quicker resolution than verdicts. They also lead to a quicker payment to the victim.

Statute of Limitations in Houston, Texas

Statute of limitations is the amount of time a victim has to begin their claim. This rule sets a time limit, usually starting at either:

  • The date of the incident that caused an injury
  • When the victim learns of their medical condition

The statute of limitations in Texas for personal injury claims is two years from the date of the injury. Claims filed in Houston follow this ruling.
